# Why Every AI Skill You Learned 6 Months Ago Is Already Wrong

**Source**: YouTube — [[Nate B Jones]], published 2026-03-01
**Link**: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RnjgLlQTMf0

## Summary

Nate B Jones names and defines the one workforce skill that can't be automated away: **frontier operations**. Using the expanding bubble metaphor (AI capability = air inside; the surface = where human judgment lives), he argues that the surface area grows as AI gets smarter — more boundary to operate at, not less. Five persistent skills, team structures, and the compounding gap between calibrated and uncalibrated workers.

See [[Frontier Operations]] for the full concept page.

## Key Claim

> "This is the first workforce skill in history that expires on a roughly quarterly cycle."

Every prior skill (literacy, coding, computer literacy) was a destination. Frontier operations has no fixed destination — the surface is always expanding.
