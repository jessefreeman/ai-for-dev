# The Hiring Arms Race — 0.4% Success Rate and Why the System Is Collapsing

## Context

From [[summary-nate-b-jones-ai-professional-interface|Nate B Jones's professional interface video]]. See also [[AI Professional Interface]].

## Content

**The arms race**: Candidates use AI to pass interviews → get hired at big salaries → get fired within a week because they can't explain their own work. Companies use AI to screen resumes → penalize candidates whose answers sound "too AI." Both sides escalate. Nobody wins.

**The numbers**:
- Application success rate: **0.4%** (not 5%, not even 4%)
- **88%** of employers admit their own systems cause them to miss qualified people
- A single engineering role: hundreds of applicants
- A product management role at a known company: 1,000+ applicants
- Recruiter spends ~6 seconds per resume

**Why optimizing harder within this system is futile**: The gates (LinkedIn, job boards, ATS) are so clogged that squeezing through at 0.4% is a finite game in a collapsing system. "You're trying to be the slightly better supplicant in a pool of 400 supplicants."

**The alternative**: Stop being in the pile. Create a different category of interaction on your own terms. The same AI that broke hiring makes this possible.

**Distribution still matters**: The interface is conversion optimization, not lead generation. You still need to get people there. But it changes what happens when they arrive.
