# SaaS Death Spiral — When the Seat Dies, So Does the 80% Margin

## Context

From [[summary-fireship-saas-death-spiral|Fireship's How AI is Breaking the SaaS Business Model]]. See also [[saas-death-spiral]] for the full analysis page synthesizing seven Feb 2026 developments.

## Content

**The data point**: Adobe, Salesforce, ServiceNow, Shopify, and other SaaS megacaps lost roughly **$1 trillion in market cap over a few weeks** in early 2026. Not interest rates, not accounting fraud — AI.

**The mechanism**: The traditional SaaS model is *rent the privilege to click buttons forever at an 80% margin*. It assumes a per-human pricing axis. When an AI agent does the work of 10 people in 10 milliseconds, the customer needs **zero seats, not ten**. The pricing axis breaks.

**Fireship's editorial throughline**:
> "When intelligence becomes abundant, software stops charging per human. And when the seat dies, so does the SaaS profit margin."
> "None of these AI companies have much of a moat. The real battle being fought right now is who can build the best platform for autonomous code orchestration."

**The seven Feb 2026 developments** that collectively made the trillion disappear:
1. OpenAI Codex Mac App ("command center for agents," 1M+ downloads week one)
2. Codex 5.3 model (25% faster, skills integration)
3. Anthropic Opus 4.6 expanding into legal/financial verticals
4. Alibaba Qwen 3 Coder Next (open weights → kills vendor lockin)
5. ZAI GLM 5 (open weights, complex systems engineering)
6. MiniMax M2.5 (frontier reasoning at fractional cost)
7. GitHub Agent HQ (Microsoft turning GitHub into a full agent orchestration platform)
8. Bonus: Waymo World Model — same primitives that obsolete SaaS dashboards

**Counter-thesis** (not in the source, added to the wiki page for balance): The death spiral hits **horizontal SaaS sold per seat to knowledge workers**, not all software business models. Vertical SaaS with proprietary data network effects (Bloomberg, Veeva, Procore) is defended by data, not seats. Orchestration platforms themselves (GitHub, Linear, Notion) become *more* valuable as agents need shared state.
