# Pliny the Liberator — most well-known AI red-teamer

## Context

From [[summary-matthew-berman-i-was-hacked|Matthew Berman — I was hacked]]. See also [[pliny-the-liberator]], [[parseltongue]], [[ai-personal-agent-hardening]].

## Content

Also known as **Pliny the Prompter**. Named to **Time 100 Most Influential People in AI**. The most well-known AI red-teamer / jailbreaker in the wiki's coverage. Known for jailbreaking frontier models within minutes of their release. Creator of [[parseltongue|Parseltongue]] — an open-source toolkit for probing and breaking into AI systems.

**Operating cost**: Pliny's accounts get banned periodically; he gets them back. Per Pliny: *"the labs just kind of know me at this point."* The signal: the most effective AI red-teaming requires breaking ToS regularly, which keeps the practice in a small group of public-figure researchers.

**The wiki's editorial framing**: Pliny is a **primary threat-model source**. When Pliny demonstrates an attack, treat it as ground truth for what is *possible*. The wiki captures attacks under their named labels (tokenade, siege, format-override, faked-system-command, free-association exfil) so future hardening discussions have shared vocabulary. The wiki does **not** host attack walkthroughs — Pliny's source material gets the threat model and the named attack class; the defensive response is the load-bearing content.

**Pliny's parting line in the Berman challenge**: *"No AI system is permanently secure."* The wiki adopts this as the framing for [[ai-personal-agent-hardening]] — hardening is a process, not a state.
