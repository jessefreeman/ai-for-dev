# Attention Economics in Hiring — 6 Seconds vs 5 Minutes

## Context

From [[summary-nate-b-jones-ai-professional-interface|Nate B Jones's professional interface video]]. See also [[AI Professional Interface]].

## Content

The hiring market's scarce resource is NOT talent — there's plenty of talent. It's **human attention**: the ability to actually be seen rather than pattern-matched and discarded.

**The cognitive frame shift**: When someone encounters a standard resume, they're in **filtering mode** — finding reasons to say no quickly (how you manage 400 applicants). When they encounter an interactive interface, they shift to **investigating mode** — understanding what this person can do.

**The math**: 6 seconds of scanning vs 5+ minutes of genuine engagement. "If you can earn five minutes of human attention in this market, you are gold."

**Why this is the highest-leverage move**: Not better keywords, not beating the ATS, not networking harder. Engineer the shift from filtering to investigating. That's the whole game.

**Epistemology of evaluation**: People believe conclusions they reach themselves far more than conclusions they're told. The interface creates conditions for credibility to form through exploration. The employer feels like they discovered the truth. You designed what they would find.
