# Three eras of AI competition

## Context

From [[summary-nate-b-jones-conway-leak|I Analyzed 512,000 Lines of Leaked Code]]. See also [[behavioral-lock-in]], [[conway]], [[anthropic]].

## Content

Nate B Jones's frame for the structural arc of frontier-AI competition:

**Era 1 (2023–24): Models.** Foundation-model competition. Benchmarks, training runs, context windows. GPT vs Claude vs Gemini. *"That race isn't over, but the margins between frontier models have compressed to the point where it's no longer the primary axis of competition."*

**Era 2 (2024–25): Interfaces.** The harness wars. Claude Code vs Cursor vs OpenClaw vs Windsurf. Who owns the surface where people actually work. **Era 2 just climaxed** with the OpenClaw ban + Steinberger's defection to OpenAI.

**Era 3 (2026): Persistence and memory.** Who owns the always-on layer that accumulates context, wakes on events, and acts autonomously. The agent that knows you not because you told it something but because it's been watching, learning, and remembering. *"All three labs (Google, Anthropic, OpenAI) have converged on the same insight — they're building the model as a loss leader and they want to own the persistent agent layer. That's the money product."*

**Why it matters**: Era 3 introduces [[behavioral-lock-in]] as a new lock-in surface that's structurally different from anything before. Whoever owns the persistence layer has customer lock-in like never before — *"not because the model is better, but because the switching cost is unthinkable."*

The wiki uses this frame to triangulate: Era 1 = the [[index|Models]] section; Era 2 = the [[claude-code|Claude Code]] / [[cursor|Cursor]] / [[openclaw|OpenClaw]] cluster; Era 3 = [[managed-agents]] + [[conway]] + the [[behavioral-lock-in]] concept page + [[open-brain|OpenBrain]] as the counter-pattern.
