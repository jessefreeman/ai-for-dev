# Google's coordinated I/O 2025 free-tools push

## Context

From [[summary-worldofai-google-jules]] and [[summary-worldofai-google-stitch]]. See also [[google]], [[saas-death-spiral]].

## Content

At **Google I/O 2025**, Google shipped two free, capable AI tools in the same week, both powered by Gemini, both targeting entrenched paid incumbents:

| Tool | Replaces | Model | Free tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| **[[jules|Google Jules]]** | [[codex|Codex]] / GitHub Copilot | Gemini 2.5 Pro | 5 tasks/day |
| **[[google-stitch|Google Stitch]]** | v0.dev / Figma | Gemini 2.5 Flash (Standard) / Pro (Experimental) | 350/mo Flash + 50/mo Pro |

This is the strongest near-term signal in the wiki's [[saas-death-spiral|SaaS death spiral]] thread. A frontier lab giving away two production-grade tools — one for code, one for design — at zero price is the kind of move that breaks per-seat pricing for everyone downstream. Codex / GitHub Copilot / Cursor / v0.dev / Figma all have to respond.

**The pattern to watch**: whether Google maintains the free pricing past launch promo periods, or whether it converts to a paid bundle once adoption stabilizes. The wiki has a [[tasks|tracking item]] for the related question of CodeLLM's 10-month-old pricing tier verification — same pattern, different vendor.

**What's distinctive about this push** vs prior Google launches:
- Both tools are **product-quality**, not research demos
- Both are **GitHub-integrated** (Jules) or **export-to-real-formats** (Stitch's Figma + HTML/CSS)
- Both run on **Gemini 2.5**, the strongest model family Google had at the time
- Both are framed in the source coverage as **direct attacks on category leaders**, not "Google also has an X"

Pairs naturally with [[firebase-studio|Firebase Studio]] (Google Cloud Next 2025) as the third leg of Google's AI-for-developers triangle: cloud IDE (Firebase Studio) + async coder (Jules) + UI designer (Stitch). All free at the entry tier, all on Gemini.
