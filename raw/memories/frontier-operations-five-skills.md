# Frontier Operations — Five Skills That Can't Be Automated

## Context

From [[summary-nate-b-jones-frontier-operations|Nate B Jones's Frontier Operations video]]. See also [[Frontier Operations]].

## Content

The expanding bubble: AI capability = air inside, human judgment = the surface. As AI gets smarter, the surface area GROWS. More boundary to operate at, not less.

**Five persistent skills:**
1. **Boundary sensing** — where does the human/agent boundary sit RIGHT NOW for your domain? Updates quarterly.
2. **Seam design** — structure handoffs between human and agent phases to be clean, verifiable, recoverable. The right seam last quarter is wrong this quarter.
3. **Failure model maintenance** — not "be skeptical" but differentiated: for task A, failure mode is X, check Y. For task B, failure mode is Z, check W.
4. **Capability forecasting** — 6-12 month bets on where the bubble expands. Like a surfer reading swells — probabilistic positioning.
5. **Leverage calibration** — where does scarce human attention create the most value? Triage 100 streams of agent output at differentiated depth.

**The compounding gap**: 6 months of calibration advantage ≠ 6 months ahead. It's worlds apart because capabilities accelerate. This explains AI-native company leverage (Cursor, Lovable, Midjourney).

**Team structures**: Team of one (high leverage individual = output of 5-10 people). Pod of five (frontier operator + specialists = output of 20).
